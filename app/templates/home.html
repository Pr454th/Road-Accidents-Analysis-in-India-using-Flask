Introduction

Road accidents in India have become a major public health concern, with thousands of people losing their lives and many more suffering injuries every year. The country has one of the highest rates of road accidents in the world, with a significant proportion of them being attributed to human error, poor infrastructure, and non-adherence to traffic rules. Analyzing road accidents in India is crucial to understand their causes and develop effective strategies to prevent them. It requires collecting and analyzing data on various factors, such as the type of vehicles involved, location, time of day, weather conditions, and driver behavior. Through such analysis, policymakers can identify patterns and trends in road accidents and devise interventions to address them. This introduction sets the stage for exploring the problem of road accidents in India and the importance of analyzing them to find solutions. By understanding the causes and consequences of road accidents, we can take steps to prevent them and create safer roads for everyone.
